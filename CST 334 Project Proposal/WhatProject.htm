What Senior Project Should I do?

Last Updated:   January 11, 2003

 

  1. In many cases a software project is trying to solve a problem.  To generate project ideas, think about what problems the following people would try to solve.

·        People doing the job I want when I graduate.

·        People working in the industry I would like to work in when I graduate,

·        People at my current job or in my current department.

 

  1. What hobbies do you have?  What tools would be useful for that hobby?  What software exists for people with this hobby?  What problems could a software program solve in this hobby?

 

  1. What data does someone you’re close to spend a lot of time tracking? 

 

    1. For example, my father is a Type II diabetic.  He keeps a careful log of his blood sugar every day.  The problem:  Real data helps everyone take him seriously and gives them more information to manage his health.  The solution:  A system to track and graph blood sugar on a PDA.  (This senior project has already been done.)

 

  1. Think about the kinds of subjects you like to talk about outside of class with friends, co-workers, parents and family, etc. Make a list of these topics.  What related software is there to these topics?  What problems could be solved?

 

  1. Who is your coolest / weirdest / most interesting friend / colleague / customer?  Talk to them.  What problems are they trying to solve? 

 

  1.  What is the ickiest job where you work? Volunteer?  Or said another way – What’s the most recent sad story you heard or told.  Could a program make it nicer? 

 

  1. Take a trip to Powell’s technical bookstore.  Glance through the case study books to prime your brainstorming pump.

 

  1. Think about what type of programming you like to do.  Look at the SIG (Special interest groups) for that topic.

 

  1. Browse around through the MIT media lab.  They have lots of cool stuff going… perhaps you can get some ideas.

 

  1. Fail. Forward. Fast.  – At this point your project doesn’t need to be perfect.  I will help you refine your proposal as time goes on.
